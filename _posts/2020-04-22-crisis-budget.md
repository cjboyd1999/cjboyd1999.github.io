## Project Introduction
Crisis Budget was an application I worked on for the 'Hack the Crisis Australia' hackathon in April. The hackathon itself was a *"48 hour virtual hackathon to find solutions that improve life, health and wellnes amidst the COVID19 outbreak."*

I joined a friend who works as a UI/UX designer to develop an app that helps users understand what financial aid they are eligible for without needing to provide any sensitive information.

As a developer and designer, we worked very well together as our skillsets compliment one another nicely. As the developer, my main task during this hackathon was to create a working prototype.

## The Work
To create a prototype desktop application, I used Python and the tkinter.ttk (Tk themed widgets) module. I chose this as a learning experience to enhance my Python knowledge, as developing a GUI was not something I had previous experience with.

### Part One: Research
After the initial brainstorm, I got straight into research what stimulus packages different countries were offering to both individuals and small businesses. As is common for hackathons, expectations of what we can deliver had to be realistic, as such, I chose three countries to research:

- Australia;
- United States of America; and
- United Kingdom.

This research would go on to serve as information for the prototype I was creating over the 48 hour period.

### Part Two: Development
With the information about what stimulus packages were available in each of the aforementioned countries, I was ready to begin developing the prototype. Fortunately, my teammate was able to produce a quick wireframe of how the application would operate. This served as a template for me to work from.

The program is simple enough in its structure as it is essentially a questionnaire that branches to different questions/responses based on a limited number of inputs.

As such, I found it was easiest to break the program up into separate Python scripts. The completed files can be found at a GitHub Gist [here](https://gist.github.com/cjboyd1999/8475225d0cc6a2d2d1c62697e14f6d10).
